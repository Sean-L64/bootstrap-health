TV Shows

Did you know?

Despite the harmful content TVs can expose, it can also be a helpful tool

Benefits

  • Educational

  • Entertaining

  • Fun activity with others

  • Relaxing

  • Reducing stress

Disadvantages

  • Addicting

  • Can reduce academic performance

  • Obesity contributor

  • Promotes unhealthy products or lifestyles

  • Early exposure to violence & aggresive behaviors

Solutions

  • Don't put a TV or other electronic devices in your room

  • Set daily TV limits 1-2 hours per day

  • Get help to choose appropriate TV programs

  • Go outside and socialize in real life

  • Criticize & analyze what's being shown

  • Constantly commuicate with close ones about what you see

  • Set a role model

  • Implement media awareness programs

  • Prioritize bedtime schedule (at least 8-10 hours)

More info

Spending more time in front of the TV allows it to gain more influence for example, heavy doses in TV violence can see increase in aggressive behavior & attitudes especially in boys. According to the National Medicine Library, the average child can witness up to 12,000 violent acts on TV anually with 1-2 hours per day impacting academic performance especially in reading. Commercials also expose kids and teens to unhealthy products and lifestyles like violence, tobacco, sex, and alcohol. It's reported that been reported that some breweries can spend up to $200 million in advertising with teens seeing 1000 - 2000 commercials with the message that "real men drink beer". Spending more time in front of the TV also prevents people from exercising, reading, and socializing in real life. However, TV in moderation and with the right programs can be a powerful teaching tool for young audiences but it's also important not to overdo it.
