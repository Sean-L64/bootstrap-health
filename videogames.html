Video Games

Did you know?

Video games can actually help with coordination and fine motor skills

Benefits

  • Develops flexible problem solving

  • Entertaining

  • Fun activity with others

  • Allows creativity

  • Improves mood

  • Develops social skills & cooperation

Disadvantages

  • Can damage mental development

  • Desensitize emotions

  • Obesity contributor

  • Cause cases of Internet Gaming Disorder (IGD)

  • Worsen physical posture

Solutions

  • Don't put a TV or other electronic devices in your room

  • Go outside and socialize in real life

  • Set time limits

  • Take breaks

  • Prioritize bedtime schedule (at least 8-10 hours)

More info

Parents should familiarize themselves with rating systems because with 1 out of 5 families have a video game gadget in a house a 2001 study found video games rated for all audiences contained significant amounts of violence where 64% contained intentional violence and 60% rewards players for injuring character. Excessive use can give people IGD(Internet Gaming Disorder) which is an addiction disorder in the Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders. The constant violence and stimuli can create stress, anxiety, mess up the dopamine rewards system, possible epileptic seizures from screen flickering, and damage posture specifically the neck and back. Video games can also cause loneliness, low self esteem and worse academic performance. However, video games can actually benefit social skills, strategizing, coordination and can even improve your mood in moderation.
